Earn your Open Badge

Upon completion of this webinar, you have the opportunity to earn either a Gold, Silver, or Bronze Open Badge indicating your competency and familiarity with using Linked Data with MARC records. By registering and attending this webinar you automatically earned your Bronze Badge that you can receive by sending an email to Jeremy Nelson and creating an Mozilla Open Badge Backpack Account.

Earning your Silver or Gold Badge

You need to answer correctly 60% of the badge assessments for a silver. To earn a Gold Badge you need to answer correctly 87% of the badge assessments. All badge assessments are in the webinar's IPython notebook, url down below.

Software Prerequisites

Please see directions on the downloads page.

Step-by-Step

If you wish to earn a Silver Badge or Gold Badge, follow these steps

  1. Visit the downloads and install IPython and dependencies.
  2. Create an Mozilla Open Badge Backpack Account
  3. Copy the webinar's IPython notebook to a local directory on your computer.
  4. Run the examples and answer all of the questions in you own copy of the notebook.
  5. After you have entered all of you answers, save the notebook and then email it as an attachment to Jeremy Nelson who will grade your notebook and email you a link to your badge and your graded IPython notebook.
  6. The email will contain directions for registering your badge in your Open Badge Backpack

Bronze Badge

The Bronze Badge is earned by attending the Coding Experiments with MARC and Linked Data in the LITA Linked Data Webinar Series.

Silver Badge

The Silver Badge is earned by scoring over 60% on the badge assessments.

Gold Badge

Earn your Gold Badge by scoring over 87% on the badge assessments.
